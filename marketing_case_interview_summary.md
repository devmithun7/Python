# Marketing Case Interview Notes (Detailed)

## Why Marketing Case Interviews Use a Framework
Marketing cases are messy: limited data, time pressure, and tradeoffs. A framework helps you structure the problem, avoid blind spots, and make defensible decisions. It also makes your thinking clear to the interviewer.

The most common structure is:
- **5C's** to understand the situation.
- **STP** to pick who to target and how to speak to them.
- **4P's** to design an actionable marketing plan.

## Typical Marketing Case Questions
- How would you market iPads to college students?
- How would you sell a new health snack?
- How would you decide which customer segment to target for a new product?

## 5C's (Situation Analysis)
**Purpose:** Build a fact base so your decision is grounded in reality. The 5C's reduce the risk of optimizing the wrong problem.

### Company
**Why it matters:** You can only win with what the company can actually do. Capabilities, brand, and goals constrain viable strategies.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Aligns the marketing plan with business goals and brand promise.
- Identifies strengths to lean into (e.g., superior UX, supply chain, pricing power).
- Surfaces constraints (budget, product roadmap, capacity).

**Key questions**
- What products does the company have?
- What competitive advantages does the company have?
- What are the company???s goals?
- What is the company???s brand image?

### Collaborators
**Why it matters:** Partners can amplify or limit reach, pricing, and customer experience.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Reveals distribution and channel leverage (retailers, platforms, affiliates).
- Clarifies margin structure and pricing flexibility.
- Highlights dependencies and negotiation risks.

**Key questions**
- Who are the company???s suppliers and distributors?
- Who are the company???s investors?
- Who has the company partnered with?
- What other relationships does the company have with third parties?

### Customers
**Why it matters:** Marketing only works if it solves a real customer problem and fits their behavior.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Shapes product messaging and value proposition.
- Prioritizes segments based on actual needs and willingness to pay.
- Avoids spending on audiences that will not convert.

**Key questions**
- Who are the company???s customers?
- What are their needs and preferences?
- What are their purchasing habits or behaviors?
- What are customer perceptions of the company?

### Competitors
**Why it matters:** You must differentiate or you compete only on price.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Identifies white space for positioning.
- Predicts competitor responses that could undermine the plan.
- Guides feature or pricing emphasis to win share.

**Key questions**
- Who are the company???s competitors?
- What are their strengths and weaknesses?
- What are their strategies and tactics?
- Who are the new potential threats?

### Context
**Why it matters:** External forces can make a strategy fail even if it looks good internally.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Ensures compliance and reduces regulatory risk.
- Anticipates shifts in demand from economic or social trends.
- Spots technology changes that create new opportunities or threats.

**Key questions**
- What are the laws and regulations in the industry?
- What are the economic trends?
- What are the new emerging technologies?
- What are social or behavioral trends?

## STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)
**Purpose:** Decide who to serve and how to win with that group. This prevents diluted messaging and wasted spend.

### Segmentation
**Why it matters:** Not all customers behave the same. Grouping them reveals which needs are worth focusing on.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Creates clear, testable segments with distinct needs.
- Allows tailored messaging and channel choices.
- Improves ROI by matching spend to responsiveness.

**Common segmentation bases**
- Needs
- Use cases
- Age
- Geography
- Income
- Lifestyle
- Attitudes

### Targeting
**Why it matters:** You must pick where to win. Trying to serve everyone usually means serving no one well.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Prioritizes the most attractive segment based on size, growth, and fit.
- Balances short-term revenue with long-term potential.
- Keeps product, pricing, and promotion aligned with a clear focus.

**Typical checks**
- Which segment is the largest?
- Which segment is growing the quickest?
- Which segment is the most profitable?
- Which segment is the most accessible?
- Which segment is the best fit for the product?
- Which segment has the most potential for improvement?
- Which segment is the most influenced by marketing?

### Positioning
**Why it matters:** Positioning clarifies why the product is the best choice for the target segment.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Guides the messaging hierarchy (what to say first, and why).
- Ensures differentiation from competitors.
- Aligns product benefits with what the target values most.

**Key questions**
- What makes this segment different from others?
- What does this segment value most?
- What are the attitudes or beliefs of this segment?

## 4P's (Marketing Mix)
**Purpose:** Convert strategy into an actionable plan the business can execute.

### Product
**Why it matters:** The product is the core of the value proposition. Marketing cannot fix poor product-market fit.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Decides which version or features to emphasize.
- Clarifies the benefits that map to the positioning.
- Guides tradeoffs when resources are limited.

**Decision prompts**
- If there are multiple products or versions, decide which to market.
- Fully understand benefits and points of differentiation for each option.
- Align the product story with the positioning statement.

### Place
**Why it matters:** Even great products fail if customers cannot find or buy them easily.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Matches channels to buying behavior and expectations.
- Optimizes distribution costs and margins.
- Identifies the channels that create the best conversion.

**Decision prompts**
- Different segments have different purchasing habits and behaviors.
- Some channels are more effective than others.
- Common channels: online, company stores, retail partners.

### Promotion
**Why it matters:** Promotion creates awareness and moves customers through the funnel.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Aligns messaging and creative with the target segment.
- Selects channels that match media habits.
- Balances short-term performance with brand building.

**Common tactics**
- Advertising
- Social media marketing
- Email marketing
- Search marketing
- Video marketing
- Public relations

### Price
**Why it matters:** Price directly impacts demand, margins, and brand perception.

**How it helps decisions:**
- Balances volume vs. profitability.
- Signals quality and positioning.
- Supports channel strategy (retail margins, promotions).

**Pricing tradeoffs to note**
- **Price too high:** demand falls and sales are lost.
- **Price too low:** customers may perceive low quality and margins shrink.

## Practical Flow in a Case
1. **5C???s** to understand the environment and constraints.
2. **STP** to pick the right customers and message.
3. **4P???s** to design and execute the marketing plan.
